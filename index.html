Project example #1

2010. Business A has invested millions in creating an ecosystem for virtual reality technology marketed towards the consumer gaming market

Due to limits of technology, the product's adoption has slowed due to poor uptake, stemming from focusing on console-style gaming controllers and other factors.


    Problems:

  • Motion detection
  • Controller
  • Multimedia


    • Solution1:

  • The primary market are PC users, rather than force them to use a clunky console-style controller:-

    • Create a product that would facilitate a more streamlined control style

      Most laptops have a webcam. Most users in the target demographic have a smartphone.

      A small curved mirror with a plastic clip to attach to the front of the webcam that will reflect the keyboard and mouse onto the camera's sensor, otherwise a stand to place a smartphone so the device camera can be used while also doubling as a normal device stand.

      A software program can crop areas of video stream around the keyboard and mouse and render as a semi-transparent and resizeable head-up display while the user wears the virtual reality headset.

      The target demographic can now wear the headset in 3-D simulators and game environments while utilizing standard controls with minimal outside distraction.

    • Cost of production of plastic clip, mirror: < $2

    • Retail price: > $10-$15

      • This would also allow game developers to create a DLC for their finished product to make them [product ready] and even charge a small fee.

      Basically just create an ability to look around the neck of the player model when user moves their head. Useful in 3-D environments, flight simulators, first-person shooters.

      In the competitive world of online gaming, quality peripherals are charged at a premium.

      Increasing the player's situational awareness with virtual reality technology while retaining the efficiency of keyboard-mouse controls.

    • Business 'A' expands sales of their technology and profits

    • 3rd-party developers renew sales of titles and profit

    • Consumers happy to purchase (product gives them an edge)

    • Technology rollout momentum increases

    • WIN-WIN-WIN
